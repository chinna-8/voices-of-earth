Climate Change Statistics

Explore critical statistics related to climate change and understand the urgency of the issue. Take a deeper dive into the numbers that tell the story of our planet’s changing climate.

Climate Change in Numbers

1.2°C

Global temperature increase since pre-industrial times. The world is on track to exceed the critical 1.5°C threshold, leading to more severe climate impacts.

Source: Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC)

18%

Percentage of global greenhouse gas emissions caused by deforestation and land-use changes. Protecting forests is key to reducing emissions.

Source: United Nations

12 Years

Time remaining to limit global warming to 1.5°C and avoid the worst impacts of climate change, according to the IPCC report. Immediate action is necessary.

Source: IPCC Special Report

0.5 mm/year

The average rate at which sea levels are rising due to global warming. Rising seas are threatening coastal communities and ecosystems.

Source: NASA Sea Level Data

40%

Percentage of the Earth's population living in coastal regions vulnerable to extreme weather events, sea level rise, and flooding.

Source: UN Environment Programme

50%+

Amount of the world's coral reefs expected to be lost by 2050 due to rising ocean temperatures, ocean acidification, and human activities.

Source: World Food Programme

$2.7 trillion

Estimated cost of climate-related disasters globally, including extreme weather, droughts, and wildfires, according to global economic forecasts.

Source: World Bank
